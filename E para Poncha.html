A Little bit of our history:

Origin of the Poncha:

Poncha is a traditional alcoholic drink from the island of Madeira. Poncha pairings usually are peanuts, lupins or small tapas known as "dentinhos".

According some studies, in the 16th Century, the discoverers transported lemon (vitamin C) in their trips, to prevent scurvy. To preserve the lemon, during the long trips, they carried it soaked in sugar cane brandy and blackstrap molasses produced in Madeira.
The first traditional poncha is called the "fisherman's poncha". The ingredients are mixed with the poncha stick, better known as “caralhinho”.
After "fisherman's poncha", was created the "Regional poncha" that is added orange juice. Nowadays, The bartenders have transformed it into a pleasant cocktail, adding various flavours like passion fruit, strawberry, peppermint, tangerine originating various flavours of poncha.
